Perspective 1486

After Ibn al-Haytham's work was translated into Latin, artists like Crivelli used the understanding of perspective to add the illusion of depth to their work.

There are plenty of inconsistencies.

But the overall perspective effect is clear.

There's space on this slide, so:

This is depicting the Annunciation, when the angel Gabriel tells Mary she will give birth to Jesus.

The perspective-incorrect light ray with a little dove represents the Holy Spirit.

Gabriel is being interrupted by Saint Emidius, who is the patron saint of Ascoli Piceno, where Crivelli live, and the location this was painted for.
